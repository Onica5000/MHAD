# Spoken Questionnaire for Audio Autofill

Read this out loud and answer each prompt in your own words, then upload the
recording on the **Snap‑to‑fill** screen ("Have a photo handy?"). The AI
transcribes what you say and fills the matching fields in your Pennsylvania
Mental Health Advance Directive. **You review every field before anything is
saved.**

> Speak naturally and completely — you don't have to use exact wording. Say
> "skip" or just move on for anything that doesn't apply. For unusual
> medication or doctor names, **say the name slowly and spell it** so it's
> transcribed correctly.

This questionnaire covers **every field the AI can fill from audio.** A short
list of things audio *can't* do (signatures, initials, dosages, a few toggles)
is at the end — handle those in the app.

> **Tip:** you don't have to record this all at once. The 60‑second processing
> limit means each clip should be **under ~2 minutes**. Record one clip per
> section below, then upload them all together — the app merges them. See
> `AUDIO_RECORDING_GUIDE.md`.

---

## 1. About you (the person this directive is for)

- State your **full legal name.**
- Your **date of birth** (month, day, year).
- Your **home address**: street and number, apartment or unit if any, city,
  county, state, and ZIP code.
- A **phone number** where you can be reached.

> Example: "My name is Jane Quincy Public. I was born March 15th, 1975. I live
> at 123 Main Street, apartment 4B, Philadelphia, Philadelphia County,
> Pennsylvania, 19103. My phone number is (215) 555‑1234."

## 2. Your doctors

- Your **primary doctor / treating physician**: their name, their specialty
  (for example psychiatry or internal medicine), and their phone number.
- If you want a **specific doctor to evaluate whether you have capacity**
  (different from your primary doctor): say their name and how to reach them.

## 3. Your mental‑health agent *(only if you're naming an agent — Combined or Power‑of‑Attorney form)*

- Your **agent's** full name, their **relationship** to you (spouse, sibling,
  friend, etc.), their **address**, and a **phone number.**
- If you have a **backup / alternate agent**, give the same details for them.

> Example: "My agent is John Public, my husband, at the same address. His cell
> phone is (215) 555‑5678. My alternate agent is Mary Public, my sister, at 50
> Oak Lane, Pittsburgh, PA, 15201, phone (412) 555‑0000."

## 4. Guardian nomination *(optional)*

- If you want to **nominate someone to be your guardian** should a court ever
  appoint one: say their name, relationship, address, and phone.

## 5. When this directive takes effect

- Describe **the circumstances that should trigger this directive** — for
  example, "when two professionals certify that I can't make my own mental
  health decisions." (This is the "when it kicks in" language — not your
  diagnoses.)

## 6. Your diagnoses

- List your **mental‑health diagnoses / conditions.** If you happen to know a
  diagnosis code (ICD‑10), you can say it, but it's optional.

> Example: "I have bipolar disorder type 1 and generalized anxiety disorder."

## 7. Medications

Say these in **four separate groups** — they go to different places on the form,
so be clear which group each medication belongs to. For each one you can add a
short **reason.**

1. **Medications I am currently taking** — what you take now, for reference.
2. **Medications I prefer** — ones that have worked well for you.
3. **Medications I never want / refuse** — ones that should not be given, and
   why.
4. **Medications with limitations** — ones that may be given but only under
   conditions you state.

> Example: "Medications I currently take: lithium and lamotrigine — spelled
> L‑A‑M‑O‑T‑R‑I‑G‑I‑N‑E. A medication I prefer is quetiapine, it has worked
> well. A medication I never want is haloperidol, it caused a severe reaction.
> A medication with limitations is clozapine — only with weekly blood
> monitoring."

> Note: exact **dosages aren't captured from audio** — add those in the app
> after autofill.

## 8. Allergies

- List your **allergies** — drug, food, or material. For each, say the
  **severity** (mild, moderate, or severe), the **reactions** it causes, and
  any **notes.**

> Example: "I'm allergic to penicillin — severe, it causes anaphylaxis. I'm
> allergic to latex — moderate, causes hives."

## 9. Treatment facility preferences

- A **hospital or treatment center you prefer** to be treated at.
- A **facility you want to avoid**, if any.

## 10. Room and environment preferences

- Any **room or environment preferences** — for example a private room, a quiet
  floor, a window, or a same‑gender roommate.

## 11. ECT, experimental studies, and drug trials

For **each** of these three, say clearly which one you want. Your choices:
**"I consent,"** **"I do not consent,"** or **"I want my agent to decide."**

- **Electroconvulsive therapy (ECT).**
- **Experimental studies / research.**
- **Drug trials.**

> Example: "For ECT, I do not consent. For experimental studies, I want my
> agent to decide. For drug trials, I do not consent."

> **Important legal note:** if you choose **"my agent decides"** for any of
> these three, Pennsylvania law (§5805(c)(4)) requires you to **physically
> initial** that authorization on the **printed** form — the recording can
> capture your choice, but it cannot initial for you.

## 12. Limits on your agent's authority *(only if naming an agent)*

- Any **limits or conditions on what your agent may or may not do** — for
  example, "my agent must consult my sister before any decision," or "my agent
  may not consent to ECT."

## 13. Additional instructions

Cover any of these that apply (say "skip" for the rest):

- **Activities** that help or worsen your symptoms.
- **What helps you during a crisis** — preferred interventions, what calms you,
  what to avoid.
- **Health history** — past hospitalizations, what treatments have or haven't
  worked.
- **Dietary** needs or restrictions.
- **Religious or cultural** preferences relevant to your care.
- **Children / custody** arrangements to be aware of.
- **Who to notify** (and who *not* to notify) — family notification.
- **Records disclosure** — who may see your records.
- **Pet care** — who cares for your pets if you're hospitalized.
- **Anything else** you want your care team and agent to know.

---

## What the recording can NOT do (finish these in the app)

The AI fills everything above, but the following are **not** capturable from
audio — set them yourself in the wizard:

- **Your signature and witness signatures.**
- **Physical initials** for granting your agent authority over ECT,
  experimental studies, or drug trials (a legal requirement — see §11).
- **Exact medication dosages** for the medications you currently take.
- **On/off authority toggles** — whether your agent may consent to your
  hospitalization, and whether your agent decides your medications.
- **The treatment‑facility "no preference" vs. specific choice** (naming a
  preferred/avoid facility in §9 is captured; the overall selection is set in
  the app).
- **Room‑preference checkboxes and same‑gender‑roommate sub‑choice** (your
  spoken note from §10 is captured as free text).
- **The structured crisis plan** (early‑warning signs, triggers, what helps,
  what to say/not say) and the **side‑effects checklist** — these are built in
  the app. (Your general crisis notes from §13 *are* captured.)
- **The self‑binding (Ulysses) clause** opt‑in.
- **Choosing your form type** (Combined, Declaration, or Power of Attorney).

Always **review every autofilled field** before saving — correct any
mis‑heard medications, conditions, names, or numbers.
