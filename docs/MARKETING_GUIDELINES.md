# Marketing Language Guidelines — PA MHAD App

## Purpose

This document provides guidelines for all public-facing descriptions of the PA MHAD
app: store listings, website copy, social media, press releases, and any other
marketing material. The goal is to accurately describe the app's functionality while
avoiding language that could trigger FDA medical device classification or imply the
app provides professional services it does not.

## FDA Compliance: Avoid Disease/Treatment Claims

The app is NOT a medical device. It is a document-preparation tool that helps users
fill out a legal form. To maintain this status and avoid FDA regulation:

### DO use:
- "Documents your mental health treatment preferences"
- "Creates legal documents under PA Act 194"
- "Helps you prepare a Mental Health Advance Directive"
- "Records your wishes for future mental health care"
- "Guided form completion for PA MHAD"
- "Step-by-step wizard for advance directive forms"
- "Generates a printable PDF of your directive"
- "Educational content about the PA MHAD process"

### DO NOT use:
- "Manages your mental health" -- implies treatment
- "Improves mental health outcomes" -- implies efficacy
- "Mental health tool/solution" -- implies therapeutic function
- "Helps treat/prevent mental health conditions"
- "Therapy app" or "mental health app" as primary descriptor
- "AI therapist" or "AI counselor"
- "Ensures your rights are protected" -- implies legal guarantee
- "Guarantees your wishes will be followed" -- no app can guarantee that
- "Clinically validated" or "evidence-based" -- implies medical device status

### Preferred Primary Descriptor:
- "PA Mental Health Advance Directive app" (full name, describes what it does)
- "Advance directive form wizard" (functional description)
- "Legal document preparation tool" (category description)

## AI Assistant Language

The AI assistant answers general questions about the PA MHAD process. It does not
provide personalized medical, legal, or therapeutic advice.

### DO:
- "AI assistant for questions about the PA MHAD process"
- "Get general information about advance directives"
- "Ask questions about form sections and terminology"
- "AI-powered Q&A about Pennsylvania advance directives"

### DO NOT:
- "AI-powered mental health support"
- "Get personalized mental health guidance"
- "AI counselor" or "AI therapist"
- "Get help with your mental health"
- "Talk to an AI about your mental health concerns"
- "Personalized recommendations for your care"

## Crisis Resources Language

The app includes crisis resources (988 Lifeline, Crisis Text Line, SAMHSA helpline).
These are links to external services, not features of the app.

### DO:
- "Includes links to crisis resources"
- "Quick access to 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline"
- "Crisis hotline numbers available in the app"

### DO NOT:
- "Provides crisis intervention"
- "Crisis management features"
- "Emergency mental health services"
- Imply the app itself provides crisis support

## Privacy Features Language

### DO:
- "Optional biometric lock for privacy"
- "All data stored locally on your device"
- "Delete all data at any time"
- "AI assistant requires explicit permission each session"

### DO NOT:
- "HIPAA-compliant" -- the app is not a covered entity
- "Medical-grade security" -- implies medical device
- "Certified secure" -- unless an actual certification is obtained

## App Store Category

- **Primary category**: Medical (for discoverability among users searching for
  health-related tools)
- **Important**: The "not a medical device" disclaimer must appear prominently in
  the store listing description to avoid user confusion about the app's purpose

## Required Disclaimers

Every public-facing description of the app MUST include all of the following:

1. **"This app is not a medical device"**
2. **"Does not provide medical, legal, or therapeutic advice"**
3. **"For adults (18+) in Pennsylvania"**
4. **"Directives must be printed, signed, and witnessed to be legally valid"**

### Placement:
- **App store listing**: First paragraph of the full description
- **Website**: Footer or dedicated disclaimer section visible on every page
- **Social media**: Include in bio/about section; link to full disclaimers
- **Press releases**: Include in boilerplate paragraph at the end
- **Screenshots/promotional images**: Not required in images, but the first
  screenshot should show the app's disclaimer screen if possible

## Review Process

All marketing copy should be reviewed against this document before publication.
When in doubt, err on the side of more conservative language. The key principle:
**describe what the app does (helps fill out a form), not what the form does
(directs medical treatment).**
